Past Continuous

Also called Present Progressive — used for actions happening now

Linked to practice: past form, affirmative, negative (didn't), and questions (Did) match the four exercise blocks. B1 Past Continuous Exercises · Module map

1. What is Past Continuous used for? 🕰️

Past Continuous describes actions that were in progress at a specific moment in the past.

  • an action happening at a specific time in the past
  • two actions happening at the same time
  • a long action interrupted by a short action
  • temporary situations in the past

Examples:

  • I was playing football at 6 pm.
  • She was studying while he was cooking.
  • They were watching TV when the phone rang.

2. Verb form in Past Continuous 🎯

Structure:

was / were + verb-ing

Examples:

  • I was playing
  • You were playing
  • We were playing
  • They were playing

Third person singular:

  • He was playing
  • She was playing
  • It was playing

The auxiliary verb was/were shows the past.

3. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓

Affirmative (+)

  • I was playing.
  • They were playing.

Negative (–)

  • I was not playing.
  • He was not playing.

Question (?)

  • Was I playing?
  • Were they playing?

Important: the verb always ends in -ing.

4. Word order in Past Continuous 🧩

Affirmative:

  • Subject + was/were + verb-ing
  • I was playing football.

Negative and questions:

  • Subject + was/were + not + verb-ing
  • She was not playing.
  • was/were + Subject + verb-ing?
  • Were you playing?

English depends on word order — the auxiliary verb must be in the correct place.

5. Time expressions used with Past Continuous ⏰

Common expressions:

  • at 6 pm – I was playing at 6 pm.
  • yesterday at this time – She was studying yesterday at this time.
  • all evening – We were playing all evening.

Interrupted actions:

  • when – I was playing when the phone rang.
  • while – They were playing while I was cooking.

These expressions show that the action was in progress in the past.

6. Spelling rules for -ing ✏️

Most verbs:

  • play → playing
  • read → reading

Verbs ending in -e:

  • make → making
  • write → writing

Short vowel + consonant → double consonant:

  • run → running
  • sit → sitting

Ending in -ie → -ying:

  • lie → lying
  • die → dying

These rules explain why “playing” looks different from “running” or “making”.
