Present Continuous

Also called Present Progressive — used for actions happening now

Linked to practice: -ing form, affirmative, negative, and questions match the four exercise blocks. B1 Present Continuous Exercises · Module map

1. Why is the verb so important? 🔑

In English, the verb is the main “switch” of the sentence — it tells us when something happens (time) and how it happens (right now, temporarily, in progress…).

  • in Slovak, we often change endings and word order
  • in English, the verb form changes the most
  • the same idea can be expressed using different tenses (present, past, future)

If you understand the form of the verb, you understand the tense of the sentence.

2. What is Present Continuous used for? ⏱️

We use Present Continuous for:

  • actions happening right now
  • temporary situations
  • actions happening around this moment
  • future arrangements (planned events)

Examples with the verb “play”:

  • I am playing football now.
  • She is playing in the garden.
  • We are playing this week.

Note: Present Continuous is also called Present Progressive. Both names mean the same tense.

3. Verb form in Present Continuous 🎯

Structure:

am / is / are + verb-ing

Examples:

  • I am playing
  • You are playing
  • We are playing
  • They are playing

Third person singular:

  • He is playing
  • She is playing
  • It is playing

The auxiliary verb changes (am/is/are), not the main verb.

4. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓

Affirmative (+)

  • I am playing.
  • He is playing.

Negative (–)

  • I am not playing.
  • He is not playing.

Question (?)

  • Am I playing?
  • Is he playing?
  • Are they playing?

Important: the verb play always becomes playing.

5. Word order matters in English 🧩

Affirmative:

  • Subject + am/is/are + Verb-ing
  • I am playing football.

Negative and questions:

  • Subject + am/is/are + not + Verb-ing
  • He is not playing.
  • Am/Is/Are + Subject + Verb-ing?
  • Are you playing?

English depends on word order — the auxiliary verb must be in the correct place.

6. Time expressions used with Present Continuous ⏰

Common expressions:

  • now – I am playing now.
  • right now – She is playing right now.
  • at the moment – We are playing at the moment.
  • today – They are playing today.

Temporary situations:

  • this week – I am playing a lot this week.
  • these days – He is playing more these days.
  • for now – We are playing for now.

These expressions show that the action is happening now or around now.

7. Spelling rules for -ing ✏️

Most verbs:

  • play → playing
  • read → reading

Verbs ending in -e:

  • make → making
  • write → writing

Short vowel + consonant → double consonant:

  • run → running
  • sit → sitting

Ending in -ie → -ying:

  • lie → lying
  • die → dying

These rules explain why “playing” looks different from “running” or “making”.
