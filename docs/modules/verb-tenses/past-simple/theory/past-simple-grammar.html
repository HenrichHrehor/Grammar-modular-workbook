Past Simple

Also called Present Progressive — used for actions happening now

Linked to practice: past form, affirmative, negative (didn't), and questions (Did) match the four exercise blocks. B1 Past Simple Exercises · Module map

1. What is Past Simple used for? 🕰️

Past Simple describes actions that are finished and happened at a specific time in the past.

  • completed actions (yesterday, last week, in 2010…)
  • past habits (when I was a child…)
  • actions in a sequence (first…, then…, after that…)
  • stories and descriptions of past events

Examples:

  • I played football yesterday.
  • She visited her grandma last weekend.
  • They watched a movie on Friday.

2. Regular and Irregular Verbs 🎯

Regular verbs:

We add -ed to form the past.

  • play → played
  • watch → watched
  • work → worked

Irregular verbs:

The form changes completely — must be memorized.

  • go → went
  • see → saw
  • have → had
  • eat → ate

3. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓

Affirmative (+)

  • I played.
  • He went.

Negative (–)

  • I did not play.
  • He did not go.

Question (?)

  • Did you play?
  • Did he go?

Important: In negative and question forms, the verb returns to the base form.

4. Word order in Past Simple 🧩

Affirmative:

  • Subject + Past Verb
  • I played football.

Negative and questions:

  • Subject + did not + Verb
  • He did not play.
  • Did + Subject + Verb?
  • Did you play?

English depends on word order — “did” must be in the correct place.

5. Time expressions used with Past Simple ⏰

Common expressions:

  • yesterday – I played yesterday.
  • last week – She visited last week.
  • last year – We travelled last year.
  • in 2010 – They moved in 2010.

Past habits:

  • when I was a child – I played outside every day.
  • back then – He worked a lot back then.
  • in the past – People lived differently in the past.

These expressions show that the action is finished and belongs to the past.

6. Spelling rules for regular verbs ✏️

Most verbs:

  • play → played
  • work → worked

Verbs ending in -e:

  • live → lived
  • love → loved

Short vowel + consonant → double consonant:

  • stop → stopped
  • plan → planned

Ending in consonant + y → -ied:

  • try → tried
  • study → studied

These rules explain why “played” looks different from “studied” or “stopped”.
