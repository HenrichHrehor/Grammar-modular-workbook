Present Simple

Simple explanation for understanding the tense (from your Present Simple source)

Linked to practice pools: Sections 1–4 below match the four exercise blocks on the practice page (B1 pool). After reading, open practice and use New set to draw fresh questions from the same topics. See the module map.

1. Why is the verb so important? 🔑

In English, the verb is the main “switch” of the sentence — it tells us when something happens (time) and how it happens (regularly, repeatedly, as a fact…).

  • in Slovak, we often change endings and word order
  • in English, the verb form changes the most
  • the same idea can be expressed using different tenses (present, past, future)

If you understand the form of the verb, you understand the tense of the sentence.

2. What is Present Simple used for? ⏱️

We use Present Simple for:

  • facts and general truths
  • habits and routines
  • things that happen regularly
  • timetables and schedules

Examples with the verb “play”:

  • I play football every Monday.
  • You play very well.
  • He plays the guitar.

3. Verb form in Present Simple 🎯

Most subjects use the base form:

  • I play
  • You play
  • We play
  • They play

Third person singular adds -s:

  • He plays
  • She plays
  • It plays

This is the most important change in Present Simple.

4. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓

Affirmative (+)

  • I play.
  • He plays.

Negative (–)

  • I do not play.
  • He does not play.

Question (?)

  • Do you play?
  • Does he play?

Important: in negative and question forms, the verb returns to the base form play.

5. Word order matters in English 🧩

English has a fixed word order:

  • Affirmative: Subject + Verb + Object
  • I play football.

Negative and questions use “do/does”:

  • Negative: Subject + do/does + not + Verb
  • He does not play.
  • Question: Do/Does + Subject + Verb?
  • Do you play?

Because English depends on word order, learning these three forms (+ / – / ?) is essential.

6. Time expressions used with Present Simple ⏰

Frequency words:

  • always – I always play in the morning.
  • sometimes – I sometimes play with my friends.
  • seldom – I seldom play computer games.
  • never – I never play late at night.

Regular time expressions:

  • every day – I play every day.
  • every Monday – I play every Monday.
  • every week – I play every week.
  • in the morning – I play in the morning.

These expressions help the student understand that Present Simple describes regular or repeated actions.

7. Spelling rules for -s / -es / -ies ✏️

Most verbs add -s:

  • play → plays
  • read → reads

Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -x, -s add -es:

  • catch → catches
  • wash → washes

Verbs ending in consonant + y change to -ies:

  • study → studies
  • try → tries

Irregular:

  • do → does
  • go → goes

These rules explain why “he plays” is different from “he studies” or “he does”.
