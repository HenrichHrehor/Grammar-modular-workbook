Passive Voice — Grammar

How active tenses connect to passive forms (be + past participle)

1. Present Simple

Active: Over 140,000 vehicles use this part of the M6.
Passive: This part of the M6 is used by over 140,000 vehicles.

The auxiliary is matches present simple use.

2. Past Simple

Active: They built the road in 1960.
Passive: The road was built in 1960.

was matches past simple built.

3. Present Perfect

Active: Engineers have repaired the bridge.
Passive: The bridge has been repaired by engineers.

has been matches present perfect have repaired.

4. Future Simple (will)

Active: They will finish the project next year.
Passive: The project will be finished next year.

will be matches future will finish.
