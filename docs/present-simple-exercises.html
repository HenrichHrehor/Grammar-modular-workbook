Present Simple Exercises

Practice affirmative, negative, and question forms

Exercise 1: Add -s/-es/-ies to the verb

Instructions: Write the correct form of the verb for "he/she/it".

1. She (play) tennis every Saturday.
2. He (watch) TV in the evening.
3. My brother (study) English at university.
4. The train (go) to London every morning.
5. She (do) her homework after school.

Exercise 2: Complete the affirmative sentences

Instructions: Use the correct form of the verb in brackets.

1. I (like) chocolate ice cream.
2. My friends (live) in a big city.
3. The shop (open) at 9 o'clock.
4. We (speak) English in class.
5. My cat (sleep) on the sofa every day.

Exercise 3: Make negative sentences

Instructions: Use "do not" or "does not" + base verb.

1. I (like) spicy food.
→ I spicy food.
2. She (play) basketball.
→ She basketball.
3. They (work) on Sundays.
→ They on Sundays.
4. He (drink) coffee.
→ He coffee.
5. My parents (watch) TV in the morning.
→ My parents TV in the morning.

Exercise 4: Make questions

Instructions: Use "Do" or "Does" + subject + base verb.

1. You speak French.
→ French?
2. She likes pizza.
→ pizza?
3. They live in London.
→ in London?
4. He works in a bank.
→ in a bank?
5. Your brother studies medicine.
→ medicine?
