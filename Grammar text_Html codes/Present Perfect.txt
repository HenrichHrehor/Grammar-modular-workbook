<!-- 1. WHAT PRESENT PERFECT IS USED FOR -->
<div class="box highlight">
  <h2>1. What is Present Perfect used for? ⏱️</h2>
  <p>Present Perfect describes actions that happened <strong>before now</strong> but are <strong>important for the present moment</strong>.</p>

  <ul class="list">
    <li>life experience (no exact time)</li>
    <li>recent events with present result</li>
    <li>actions that started in the past and continue now</li>
  </ul>

  <p><strong>Examples:</strong></p>
  <ul class="list">
    <li><span class="formula">I have played football many times.</span> (experience)</li>
    <li><span class="formula">She has just finished her homework.</span> (recent result)</li>
    <li><span class="formula">We have lived here for 10 years.</span> (still true now)</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<!-- 2. FORM OF THE VERB -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>2. Verb form in Present Perfect 🎯</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Structure:</strong></p>
      <p><span class="formula">have / has + past participle</span></p>

      <p><strong>Examples with “play”:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I have played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">You have played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">We have played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">They have played</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Third person singular:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">He has played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">She has played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">It has played</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p>The auxiliary verb <strong>have/has</strong> shows the tense.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 3. REGULAR AND IRREGULAR PAST PARTICIPLES -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>3. Regular and Irregular Past Participles 📘</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Regular verbs:</strong></p>
      <p>Past participle = <strong>-ed</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">play → played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">watch → watched</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">work → worked</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Irregular verbs:</strong></p>
      <p>Must be memorized.</p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">go → gone</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">see → seen</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">eat → eaten</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">write → written</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 4. AFFIRMATIVE / NEGATIVE / QUESTION -->
<div class="box" style="page-break-before: always; break-before: page;">
  <h2>4. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative (+)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I have played.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He has played.</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Negative (–)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I have not played.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">She has not played.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Question (?)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">Have you played?</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">Has he played?</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Important:</strong> the main verb is always in the <strong>past participle</strong> form.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 5. WORD ORDER -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>5. Word order in Present Perfect 🧩</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Subject + have/has + Past Participle</li>
        <li><span class="formula">I have played football.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Negative and questions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Subject + have/has + not + Past Participle</li>
        <li><span class="formula">He has not played.</span></li>
        <li>Have/Has + Subject + Past Participle?</li>
        <li><span class="formula">Have you played?</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>English depends on word order — “have/has” must be in the correct place.</p>
</div>

<!-- 6. TIME EXPRESSIONS -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>6. Time expressions used with Present Perfect ⏰</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Experience:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>ever – <span class="formula">Have you ever played tennis?</span></li>
        <li>never – <span class="formula">I have never played golf.</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Recent events:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>just – <span class="formula">She has just finished.</span></li>
        <li>already – <span class="formula">We have already played.</span></li>
        <li>yet – <span class="formula">Have you played yet?</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Actions continuing until now:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>for – <span class="formula">I have played for 10 years.</span></li>
        <li>since – <span class="formula">He has played since 2015.</span></li>
        <li>so far – <span class="formula">We have played three games so far.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These expressions show that Present Perfect connects the past with the present.</p>
</div>

<!-- 7. SPELLING RULES FOR REGULAR PAST PARTICIPLES -->
<div class="box" style="page-break-before: always; break-before: page;">
  <h2>7. Spelling rules for regular past participles ✏️</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Most verbs:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">play → played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">work → worked</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Verbs ending in -e:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">live → lived</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">love → loved</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Short vowel + consonant → double consonant:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">stop → stopped</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">plan → planned</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Ending in consonant + y → -ied:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">try → tried</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">study → studied</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These rules explain why “played” looks different from “studied” or “stopped”.</p>
</div>
