<!-- 1. WHY THE VERB MATTERS -->
<div class="box highlight">
  <h2>1. Why is the verb so important? 🔑</h2>
  <p>In English, the <strong>verb is the main “switch” of the sentence</strong> — it tells us <strong>when</strong> something happens (time) and <strong>how</strong> it happens (right now, temporarily, in progress…).</p>
  <ul class="list">
    <li>in Slovak, we often change endings and word order</li>
    <li>in English, the <strong>verb form changes the most</strong></li>
    <li>the same idea can be expressed using different tenses (present, past, future)</li>
  </ul>
  <p>If you understand the <strong>form of the verb</strong>, you understand the <strong>tense of the sentence</strong>.</p>
</div>

<!-- 2. WHAT PRESENT CONTINUOUS IS USED FOR -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>2. What is Present Continuous used for? ⏱️</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>We use Present Continuous for:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>actions happening right now</li>
        <li>temporary situations</li>
        <li>actions happening around this moment</li>
        <li>future arrangements (planned events)</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Examples with the verb “play”:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I am playing football now.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">She is playing in the garden.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">We are playing this week.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p><strong>Note:</strong> Present Continuous is also called <strong>Present Progressive</strong>. Both names mean the same tense.</p>
</div>

<!-- 3. FORM OF THE VERB (am/is/are + playing) -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>3. Verb form in Present Continuous 🎯</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Structure:</strong></p>
      <p><span class="formula">am / is / are + verb-ing</span></p>

      <p><strong>Examples:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I am playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">You are playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">We are playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">They are playing</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Third person singular:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">He is playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">She is playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">It is playing</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p>The auxiliary verb <strong>changes</strong> (am/is/are), not the main verb.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 4. AFFIRMATIVE / NEGATIVE / QUESTION -->
<div class="box" style="page-break-before: always; break-before: page;">

  <h2>4. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative (+)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I am playing.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He is playing.</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Negative (–)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I am not playing.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He is not playing.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Question (?)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">Am I playing?</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">Is he playing?</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">Are they playing?</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Important:</strong> the verb <span class="formula">play</span> always becomes <span class="formula">playing</span>.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 5. WORD ORDER IN PRESENT CONTINUOUS -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>5. Word order matters in English 🧩</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Subject + am/is/are + Verb-ing</li>
        <li><span class="formula">I am playing football.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Negative and questions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Subject + am/is/are + not + Verb-ing</li>
        <li><span class="formula">He is not playing.</span></li>
        <li>Am/Is/Are + Subject + Verb-ing?</li>
        <li><span class="formula">Are you playing?</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>English depends on word order — the auxiliary verb must be in the correct place.</p>
</div>

<!-- 6. TIME EXPRESSIONS FOR PRESENT CONTINUOUS -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>6. Time expressions used with Present Continuous ⏰</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Common expressions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>now – <span class="formula">I am playing now.</span></li>
        <li>right now – <span class="formula">She is playing right now.</span></li>
        <li>at the moment – <span class="formula">We are playing at the moment.</span></li>
        <li>today – <span class="formula">They are playing today.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Temporary situations:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>this week – <span class="formula">I am playing a lot this week.</span></li>
        <li>these days – <span class="formula">He is playing more these days.</span></li>
        <li>for now – <span class="formula">We are playing for now.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These expressions show that the action is happening now or around now.</p>
</div>

<!-- 7. SPELLING RULES FOR -ING -->
<div class="box" style="page-break-before: always; break-before: page;">

  <h2>7. Spelling rules for -ing ✏️</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Most verbs:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">play → playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">read → reading</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Verbs ending in -e:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">make → making</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">write → writing</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Short vowel + consonant → double consonant:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">run → running</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">sit → sitting</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Ending in -ie → -ying:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">lie → lying</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">die → dying</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These rules explain why “playing” looks different from “running” or “making”.</p>
</div>
