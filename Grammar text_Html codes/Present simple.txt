<!-- 1. WHY THE VERB MATTERS -->
<div class="box highlight">
  <h2>1. Why is the verb so important? 🔑</h2>
  <p>In English, the <strong>verb is the main “switch” of the sentence</strong> — it tells us <strong>when</strong> something happens (time) and <strong>how</strong> it happens (regularly, repeatedly, as a fact…).</p>
  <ul class="list">
    <li>in Slovak, we often change endings and word order</li>
    <li>in English, the <strong>verb form changes the most</strong></li>
    <li>the same idea can be expressed using different tenses (present, past, future)</li>
  </ul>
  <p>If you understand the <strong>form of the verb</strong>, you understand the <strong>tense of the sentence</strong>.</p>
</div>

<!-- 2. WHAT PRESENT SIMPLE IS USED FOR -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>2. What is Present Simple used for? ⏱️</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>We use Present Simple for:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>facts and general truths</li>
        <li>habits and routines</li>
        <li>things that happen regularly</li>
        <li>timetables and schedules</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Examples with the verb “play”:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I play football every Monday.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">You play very well.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He plays the guitar.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 3. FORM OF THE VERB (play / plays) -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>3. Verb form in Present Simple 🎯</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Most subjects use the base form:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I play</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">You play</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">We play</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">They play</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Third person singular adds -s:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">He plays</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">She plays</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">It plays</span></li>
      </ul>
      <p>This is the most important change in Present Simple.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 4. AFFIRMATIVE / NEGATIVE / QUESTION -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>4. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative (+)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I play.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He plays.</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Negative (–)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I do not play.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He does not play.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Question (?)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">Do you play?</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">Does he play?</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Important:</strong> in negative and question forms, the verb returns to the base form <span class="formula">play</span>.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 5. WORD ORDER IN PRESENT SIMPLE -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>5. Word order matters in English 🧩</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>English has a fixed word order:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Affirmative:</strong> Subject + Verb + Object</li>
        <li><span class="formula">I play football.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Negative and questions use “do/does”:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Negative:</strong> Subject + do/does + not + Verb</li>
        <li><span class="formula">He does not play.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Question:</strong> Do/Does + Subject + Verb?</li>
        <li><span class="formula">Do you play?</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>Because English depends on word order, learning these three forms (+ / – / ?) is essential.</p>
</div>

<!-- 6. TIME EXPRESSIONS FOR PRESENT SIMPLE -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>6. Time expressions used with Present Simple ⏰</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Frequency words:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>always – <span class="formula">I always play in the morning.</span></li>
        <li>sometimes – <span class="formula">I sometimes play with my friends.</span></li>
        <li>seldom – <span class="formula">I seldom play computer games.</span></li>
        <li>never – <span class="formula">I never play late at night.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Regular time expressions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>every day – <span class="formula">I play every day.</span></li>
        <li>every Monday – <span class="formula">I play every Monday.</span></li>
        <li>every week – <span class="formula">I play every week.</span></li>
        <li>in the morning – <span class="formula">I play in the morning.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These expressions help the student understand that Present Simple describes regular or repeated actions.</p>
</div>

<!-- 7. SPELLING RULES FOR THIRD PERSON SINGULAR -->
<div class="box example">
  <h2>7. Spelling rules for -s / -es / -ies ✏️</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Most verbs add -s:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">play → plays</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">read → reads</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -x, -s add -es:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">catch → catches</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">wash → washes</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Verbs ending in consonant + y change to -ies:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">study → studies</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">try → tries</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Irregular:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">do → does</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">go → goes</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These rules explain why “he plays” is different from “he studies” or “he does”.</p>
</div>
