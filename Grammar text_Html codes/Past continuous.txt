    <h1>Past Continuous</h1>
    <div class="subtitle">also called Past Progressive – used for actions in progress in the past</div>

    <div class="grid">

<!-- 1. WHAT PAST CONTINUOUS IS USED FOR -->
<div class="box highlight">
  <h2>1. What is Past Continuous used for? 🕰️</h2>
  <p>Past Continuous describes actions that were <strong>in progress</strong> at a <strong>specific moment in the past</strong>.</p>

  <ul class="list">
    <li>an action happening at a specific time in the past</li>
    <li>two actions happening at the same time</li>
    <li>a long action interrupted by a short action</li>
    <li>temporary situations in the past</li>
  </ul>

  <p><strong>Examples:</strong></p>
  <ul class="list">
    <li><span class="formula">I was playing football at 6 pm.</span></li>
    <li><span class="formula">She was studying while he was cooking.</span></li>
    <li><span class="formula">They were watching TV when the phone rang.</span></li>
  </ul>
</div>

<!-- 2. FORM OF THE VERB -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>2. Verb form in Past Continuous 🎯</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Structure:</strong></p>
      <p><span class="formula">was / were + verb-ing</span></p>

      <p><strong>Examples:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I was playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">You were playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">We were playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">They were playing</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Third person singular:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">He was playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">She was playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">It was playing</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p>The auxiliary verb <strong>was/were</strong> shows the past.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 3. AFFIRMATIVE / NEGATIVE / QUESTION -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>3. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative (+)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I was playing.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">They were playing.</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Negative (–)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I was not playing.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He was not playing.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Question (?)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">Was I playing?</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">Were they playing?</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Important:</strong> the verb always ends in <span class="formula">-ing</span>.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 4. WORD ORDER -->
<div class="box" style="page-break-before: always; break-before: page;">
  <h2>4. Word order in Past Continuous 🧩</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Subject + was/were + verb-ing</li>
        <li><span class="formula">I was playing football.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Negative and questions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Subject + was/were + not + verb-ing</li>
        <li><span class="formula">She was not playing.</span></li>
        <li>was/were + Subject + verb-ing?</li>
        <li><span class="formula">Were you playing?</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>English depends on word order — the auxiliary verb must be in the correct place.</p>
</div>

<!-- 5. TIME EXPRESSIONS -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>5. Time expressions used with Past Continuous ⏰</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Common expressions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>at 6 pm – <span class="formula">I was playing at 6 pm.</span></li>
        <li>yesterday at this time – <span class="formula">She was studying yesterday at this time.</span></li>
        <li>all evening – <span class="formula">We were playing all evening.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Interrupted actions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>when – <span class="formula">I was playing when the phone rang.</span></li>
        <li>while – <span class="formula">They were playing while I was cooking.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These expressions show that the action was in progress in the past.</p>
</div>

<!-- 6. SPELLING RULES FOR -ING -->
<div class="box example">
  <h2>6. Spelling rules for -ing ✏️</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Most verbs:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">play → playing</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">read → reading</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Verbs ending in -e:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">make → making</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">write → writing</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Short vowel + consonant → double consonant:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">run → running</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">sit → sitting</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Ending in -ie → -ying:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">lie → lying</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">die → dying</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These rules explain why “playing” looks different from “running” or “making”.</p>
</div>
