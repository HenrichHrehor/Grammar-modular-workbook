<!-- 1. WHAT PAST SIMPLE IS USED FOR -->
<div class="box highlight">
  <h2>1. What is Past Simple used for? 🕰️</h2>
  <p>Past Simple describes actions that are <strong>finished</strong> and happened at a <strong>specific time in the past</strong>.</p>

  <ul class="list">
    <li>completed actions (yesterday, last week, in 2010…)</li>
    <li>past habits (when I was a child…)</li>
    <li>actions in a sequence (first…, then…, after that…)</li>
    <li>stories and descriptions of past events</li>
  </ul>

  <p><strong>Examples:</strong></p>
  <ul class="list">
    <li><span class="formula">I played football yesterday.</span></li>
    <li><span class="formula">She visited her grandma last weekend.</span></li>
    <li><span class="formula">They watched a movie on Friday.</span></li>
  </ul>
</div>

<!-- 2. REGULAR AND IRREGULAR VERBS -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>2. Regular and Irregular Verbs 🎯</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Regular verbs:</strong></p>
      <p>We add <strong>-ed</strong> to form the past.</p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">play → played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">watch → watched</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">work → worked</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Irregular verbs:</strong></p>
      <p>The form changes completely — must be memorized.</p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">go → went</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">see → saw</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">have → had</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">eat → ate</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 3. AFFIRMATIVE / NEGATIVE / QUESTION -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>3. Affirmative, Negative and Question forms ➕➖❓</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative (+)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I played.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He went.</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Negative (–)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">I did not play.</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">He did not go.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Question (?)</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">Did you play?</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">Did he go?</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Important:</strong> In negative and question forms, the verb returns to the <strong>base form</strong>.</p>
    </div>

  </div>
</div>

<!-- 4. WORD ORDER -->
<div class="box" style="page-break-before: always; break-before: page;">

  <h2>4. Word order in Past Simple 🧩</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Affirmative:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Subject + Past Verb</li>
        <li><span class="formula">I played football.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Negative and questions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Subject + did not + Verb</li>
        <li><span class="formula">He did not play.</span></li>
        <li>Did + Subject + Verb?</li>
        <li><span class="formula">Did you play?</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>English depends on word order — “did” must be in the correct place.</p>
</div>

<!-- 5. TIME EXPRESSIONS -->
<div class="box">
  <h2>5. Time expressions used with Past Simple ⏰</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Common expressions:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>yesterday – <span class="formula">I played yesterday.</span></li>
        <li>last week – <span class="formula">She visited last week.</span></li>
        <li>last year – <span class="formula">We travelled last year.</span></li>
        <li>in 2010 – <span class="formula">They moved in 2010.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Past habits:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li>when I was a child – <span class="formula">I played outside every day.</span></li>
        <li>back then – <span class="formula">He worked a lot back then.</span></li>
        <li>in the past – <span class="formula">People lived differently in the past.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These expressions show that the action is finished and belongs to the past.</p>
</div>

<!-- 6. SPELLING RULES FOR REGULAR VERBS -->
<div class="box example">
  <h2>6. Spelling rules for regular verbs ✏️</h2>

  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:8px;">

    <div>
      <p><strong>Most verbs:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">play → played</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">work → worked</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Verbs ending in -e:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">live → lived</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">love → loved</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div>
      <p><strong>Short vowel + consonant → double consonant:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">stop → stopped</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">plan → planned</span></li>
      </ul>

      <p><strong>Ending in consonant + y → -ied:</strong></p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><span class="formula">try → tried</span></li>
        <li><span class="formula">study → studied</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p>These rules explain why “played” looks different from “studied” or “stopped”.</p>
</div>
